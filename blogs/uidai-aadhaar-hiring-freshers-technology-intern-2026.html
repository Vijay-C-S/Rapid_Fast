Official Instructions for Internship Applications - 2026

Below is the practical summary of the official instruction points shared for UIDAI internship applications. Read this carefully before filling the form because many candidates lose shortlist chances by missing small policy details.

  1. UIDAI invites applications from eligible candidates for Internship at UIDAI Technology Centre, Bengaluru.
  2. Applications are accepted from 01-04-2026 to 15-04-2026 through the official form.
  3. Preference is given to candidates applying for minimum 6 months tenure.
  4. UIDAI may reject any or all applications or cancel the selection process at any stage for administrative reasons.
  5. Meeting basic eligibility does not guarantee interview. If applications are high in volume, shortlisting may be merit-based or requirement-based.
  6. Any false or concealed information can lead to immediate rejection and further action.
  7. Candidates should read official Internship Policy and PPO Policy documents before applying.

This is not a casual application process. UIDAI has clearly mentioned policy-based selection and the right to shortlist based on organizational needs. So your strategy should be quality-first, not form-first.

Who Should Apply and Who Should Skip

You should strongly consider applying if your profile matches at least one of these realities:

  • You are from one of the listed disciplines and can show practical academic or project depth.
  • You can commit to at least 6 months, because tenure preference is explicitly stated.
  • You can explain your skills clearly with examples, not just keywords in a resume.
  • You want experience in high-scale digital infrastructure and public-sector technology systems.

You may skip this cycle if you cannot commit to the timeline, cannot support your claims with project evidence, or are applying only to collect internship tags without real interest in the role. In high-volume intake, shallow profiles are usually filtered early.

How UIDAI Shortlisting Usually Works in High-Volume Intake

UIDAI has already stated that essential qualification is only a baseline and does not guarantee interview. This single line is very important. It means selection quality depends on merit evidence and organizational fit. When many forms are submitted, shortlisting can happen in a layered way:

  • Academic fit check against the listed discipline.
  • Skill relevance check based on role demand in each vertical.
  • Tenure preference check, where longer commitment can help.
  • Credibility check for claims in resume, portfolio, and form responses.

So instead of asking, "Am I eligible?" ask this better question: "Can my profile survive a merit shortlisting round where 100 similar forms are submitted?" If the answer is uncertain, improve your application quality before final submission.

Resume Strategy for UIDAI Internship

Your resume must be sharp, honest, and role-mapped. Avoid writing generic statements like "hardworking and passionate." Those lines do not help in technical screening. Use this simple structure:

  • Headline: Include your discipline and strongest skill area, for example "BTech Candidate - Full Stack and Data Engineering."
  • Project Section: Mention 2 or 3 projects with problem, your role, tech used, and measurable output.
  • Skill Section: Keep only real skills you can defend in discussion.
  • Design Candidates: Add case studies, process snapshots, and links to Figma work.
  • Math/Stat Candidates: Mention modeling, analysis, or research projects with clear methods and outcomes.

Use plain language and remove inflated claims. A realistic resume always performs better than an over-decorated one in institutional hiring contexts.

Portfolio and Project Ideas That Match UIDAI Requirements

If you want better shortlist chances, align one visible project directly with the skill map in the notice. You do not need ten unfinished repositories. You need one clear and complete project story.

Examples by profile:

  • Frontend / Full Stack: Build a citizen service interface with secure form validation, responsive design, and accessibility checks.
  • Data Engineering: Build a small pipeline that ingests raw records, validates schema, and creates analytics-ready outputs.
  • AI/ML: Create a model-driven document classification workflow with clear evaluation metrics.
  • Android/iOS: Build a utility app with offline-first behavior and robust error states.
  • UI/UX: Create end-to-end design documentation: user research, IA, wireframes, prototypes, and final design system components.
  • Math/Stat: Publish a clean notebook showing statistical analysis, assumptions, and interpretation of results.

For each project, write what challenge you solved, what choices you made, and what you improved after testing. These are the details that make reviewers trust your profile.

30-Day Preparation Plan Before Interview Calls

If you are applying in this cycle, this 30-day plan can help you become interview-ready without panic:

  • Day 1-5: finalize resume, clean portfolio links, and map your skill to one discipline bucket from the notice.
  • Day 6-10: strengthen core technical fundamentals based on your track.
  • Day 11-16: build or refine one flagship project with better documentation and screenshots.
  • Day 17-21: rehearse project explanation in simple language and record mock answers.
  • Day 22-26: revise domain basics: UI/UX process, coding standards, data workflows, or statistical reasoning.
  • Day 27-30: simulate interview rounds with peers and prepare clear responses around your decisions.

The biggest difference between selected and rejected candidates is usually not talent gap. It is preparation quality and clarity under pressure.
