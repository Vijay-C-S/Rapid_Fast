Role Tracks and Expected Day-to-Day Work

One of the best parts of this internship is role diversity. The job description includes multiple technical tracks. Depending on your assigned team, you may work in one primary track and collaborate with another.

1. Technical Support

  • Assist in troubleshooting hardware and software issues.
  • Support basic network connectivity checks and system configuration tasks.
  • Help IT teams in managing and tracking company devices.

If your basics are good in OS, networking fundamentals, and diagnostic thinking, this track can give you strong real-world support experience.

2. Software Development

  • Write basic code or scripts for internal tools and project modules.
  • Support bug testing and defect validation process.
  • Participate in code review learning, version control, and secure coding practices.

This is ideal for students who want software engineering exposure in a structured enterprise environment and want to move toward SDE or product roles in future.

3. Data Analysis and Reporting

  • Assist in data collection, cleaning, and basic analysis workflows.
  • Prepare reports, dashboards, and summary visualizations using office tools or analytics templates.

If you are comfortable with Excel, basic SQL logic, and storytelling with data, this track can become a strong portfolio point for analyst and operations roles.

4. Networking and Infrastructure Support

  • Support installation and configuration of network hardware.
  • Monitor network health and report potential performance issues.
  • Assist with troubleshooting infrastructure and security-related concerns.

This track is useful for students interested in system administration, cloud infrastructure, enterprise IT operations, and network reliability engineering.

5. DevSecOps and Cloud Support

  • Assist with automation of routine tasks using scripts or tools.
  • Support cloud infrastructure management tasks.
  • Contribute to CI/CD pipeline support and deployment activities.

Students preparing for modern engineering roles should pay attention here. Even basic exposure to automation and deployment culture can make your profile much stronger than generic fresher resumes.

Resume Strategy for Unisys Student Tech Intern

Most students lose opportunities because resumes are generic. For this role, your resume should look practical, concise, and role-aligned.

  • Keep your resume to one page with clear sections: skills, projects, education, certifications.
  • Add at least one project each from support/development/data perspective if possible.
  • Mention outcomes, not only tools. Example: "Reduced script runtime by 20%" is better than "Worked on Python".
  • Ensure GitHub and LinkedIn links are active and professional.
  • Use internship keywords naturally: troubleshooting, scripting, cloud support, CI/CD, reporting.

Recruiters spend limited time in first screening. A clean, focused resume can change your result even before interviews begin.

Interview Preparation Plan (7 Days Practical Roadmap)

If you receive screening mail or assessment invite, do not panic. Use this compact preparation strategy:

  • Day 1: Revise computer fundamentals, networking basics, and OS concepts.
  • Day 2: Practice 2 small scripting problems and explain your approach aloud.
  • Day 3: Revise one project deeply: architecture, bug faced, fix done, outcome.
  • Day 4: Prepare support scenarios: slow system, login issue, network drop, permission problem.
  • Day 5: Review data reporting basics and simple dashboard logic.
  • Day 6: Practice HR and behavioral answers in concise format.
  • Day 7: Mock interview with friend, finalize resume talking points, sleep on time.

Keep your answers simple and structured: problem, action, result, learning. Interviewers appreciate clarity more than buzzwords.

Common Mistakes to Avoid Before Applying

  • Applying with outdated resume and broken project links.
  • Copy-paste objective statements that look generic and non-technical.
  • Ignoring spelling and grammar mistakes in resume or application form.
  • Listing too many skills without one strong proof project.
  • Not preparing for support questions because of overfocus on coding only.
  • Applying late and missing recruiter response windows.
